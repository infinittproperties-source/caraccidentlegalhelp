Total Loss Recovery — Know Your Rights
✦ Actual Cash Value (ACV) — insurer must pay fair market value, not book value. Pull your own comps.
✦ Illinois Sales Tax — required to be included in total loss payouts under IL law.
✦ Title & Registration Fees — recoverable when replacing a totaled vehicle.
✦ Rental Car — you're entitled from the date of accident until settlement check clears.
✦ GAP Insurance — if you owe more than ACV, check your policy immediately. Without GAP you're liable for the difference.
✦ Personal Property — car seat, phone, sunglasses, etc. damaged in crash are recoverable.

Critical Legal Deadlines

Illinois law is unforgiving. Missing a deadline can permanently extinguish your right to compensation.

Preserve dashcam & surveillance footage
Send written preservation letters to businesses & the at-fault driver's insurer. Footage is overwritten in 24–72 hours.
⚡ Do this today
Seek medical attention & document injuries
Gaps in treatment are used by insurers to argue your injuries aren't serious. See a doctor now even if you feel OK.
⚡ Within 24–48 hours
Do NOT give a recorded statement to the adverse insurer
You have no legal obligation to give one. Adjusters are trained to extract admissions that reduce your claim.
⚡ Immediately — refuse all requests
File police report (if not done)
Required under 625 ILCS 5/11-408 if injuries or damage exceed $1,500. Get the full narrative report, not just crash summary.
Within days
Notify your own insurance company
Most policies require prompt notice. Do not give recorded statements to your insurer either without attorney review.
Within 1 week
Retain a personal injury attorney
Attorneys recover 3–3.5× more than self-represented claimants. All top IL firms work on contingency — zero upfront cost.
Within 30 days
File personal injury lawsuit

Illinois Law Quick Reference
Key Statutes
735 ILCS 5/2-1116
Modified comparative negligence — 51% bar rule
You can recover if ≤50% at fault. Damages reduced proportionally. 51%+ = no recovery.
735 ILCS 5/13-202
2-year statute of limitations — personal injury
Tolled for minors until age 18. Strict deadline — missing it eliminates your claim entirely.
625 ILCS 5/11-408
Duty to report accidents to police
Required when injuries occur or property damage exceeds $1,500.
625 ILCS 5/12-610.2
Texting while driving — illegal in Illinois
Subpoena cell phone records to prove distracted driving. Key for liability.
625 ILCS 5/3-707
Driving without insurance — Class A misdemeanor
If at-fault driver is uninsured, pursue your own UM/UIM coverage immediately.
755 ILCS 5/27-6
Illinois Survival Act — wrongful death claims
Estate can continue/initiate claims the deceased had accrued before death.
